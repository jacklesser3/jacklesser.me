# Marathon Training App - User Guide

## 🏃‍♀️ How to Track Your Progress

### Quick Start

1. **Access the App**
   - Go to: https://jacklesser.me/marathon
   - Complete the onboarding questionnaire (one time only)
   - Your personalized plan is generated automatically

2. **Daily Workflow**
   - Open the dashboard
   - See today's workout at the top
   - After completing your run, log it immediately

---

## 📝 How to Log a Workout

### Method 1: From Today's Workout (Easiest)

1. Complete your run/workout
2. Open the dashboard
3. Click **"Mark Complete"** button in the "Today's Workout" card
4. Fill in the form:
   - **Distance**: How many miles you actually ran
   - **Duration**: How many minutes it took
   - **Effort Level**: Slider from 1-10 (how hard it felt)
   - **Notes** (optional): "Felt great!" or "Struggled with pace"
5. Click **"Complete Workout"**

**Your progress updates automatically!**

### Method 2: From Weekly View

1. Scroll to the week schedule (horizontal scrolling cards)
2. **Click on any workout card** to expand it
3. Click **"Log Workout"** button
4. Fill in the same form as above
5. Submit

### Visual Confirmation

When a workout is logged:
- ✓ Checkmark appears on the card
- Card turns light green
- Progress stats update (Total Miles, Completion Rate, Streak)
- Progress bar moves forward

## 📊 Understanding Your Dashboard

### Top Section - Race Countdown
- **Weeks/Days countdown**: Time until NYC Marathon
- **Progress bar**: How far through training you are
- **"Week X of Y"**: Current training week

### Today's Workout
- Shows exactly what to do today
- Quick log button
- If it's a rest day, it tells you to rest!

### Progress Stats
- **Total Miles**: All miles you've logged
- **Completion Rate**: % of workouts completed
- **Current Streak**: Consecutive workouts completed

### Week Schedule (Horizontal Scroll)
- Swipe/scroll horizontally to see all 7 days
- **Click any day** to see details
- **Today** has an orange border with • dot
- **Completed** workouts have ✓ checkmark

## 💡 Tips for Success

### Daily Habits
1. **Log immediately after running** (while you remember the details)
2. **Check dashboard daily** to see upcoming workouts
3. **Add notes** to track how you felt - helpful for patterns

### Weekly Review
- Look at completion rate
- See total mileage for the week
- Adjust effort if consistently too hard/easy

### What Gets Tracked
- Every run distance and time
- Your pace (calculated automatically)
- Effort level (subjective 1-10)
- Personal notes
- Streaks and milestones

### Data Persistence
- Everything saves to your browser automatically
- Don't clear browser data or you'll lose your progress
- Use the same device/browser for consistency
- **Coming Soon**: Export data to backup

## 🔄 Week-by-Week Flow

### Sunday
- **Long Run Day** (longest run of the week)
- Take your time, run slow
- Fuel if over 90 minutes
- Log when done

### Monday-Saturday
- Mix of easy runs, rest days, tempo runs, intervals
- Check "Today's Workout" each morning
- Complete as scheduled
- Log after each workout

### Every 4th Week
- **Recovery Week** - Lower mileage
- Let your body adapt
- Don't skip these!

## ❓ Common Questions

### "What if I miss a workout?"
- It's okay! Life happens
- Log the workouts you DO complete
- Your completion rate reflects reality
- Don't try to "make up" missed long runs

### "Can I log a workout from yesterday?"
- Yes! Click on that day's card
- Fill in the details you remember
- It will mark as completed

### "What if I ran different distance than planned?"
- Log what you ACTUALLY ran
- The plan is a guide, not a rule
- Actual distance appears on the card

### "How do I see past weeks?"
- All weeks are visible in your plan
- Scroll down on dashboard
- Completed workouts stay marked

### "What if I want to change a logged workout?"
- Currently: Click the workout card again
- It will show "Completed" status
- **Future feature**: Edit/delete logged workouts

## 🆘 Troubleshooting

### "I can't see my plan"
- Make sure you completed onboarding
- Check you're on the same browser/device
- Try refreshing the page

### "My progress didn't save"
- Browser data might have been cleared
- Make sure you clicked "Complete Workout"
- Check that the checkmark appeared

### "Calendar export isn't working"
- Click "Export to Calendar"
- A `.ics` file will download
- Open Google Calendar → Settings → Import
- Select the downloaded file

## 📅 Calendar Export Feature

### Why Use It?
- Get reminders on your phone
- See workouts in your regular calendar
- Plan around other commitments

### How to Export
1. Dashboard → "Export to Calendar" section
2. Click the button
3. File downloads: `nyc-marathon-2026-training.ics`
4. Import to your calendar app

### What You Get
- All workouts for all weeks
- Race day event
- Reminders 12 hours before each workout
- Updates if you regenerate plan
