01 — The Gap
What Eastern & Western Science Each Lack

Western Science

  • Learns about the average brain, not your brain
  • Requires instruments — can measure matter, not mind
  • Science is verifiable & transmissible
  • Does things to you (surgery, medication)
  • Gap: can’t detect subjective experience

Eastern Traditions

  • The only tool that can observe mind is mind itself
  • All DIY — no one can do it for you
  • Practices are personalized and experiential
  • No referral system between gurus
  • Gap: no external way to verify claims
“A yogi sits in the Himalayas, doesn't talk to anyone, and he has the one tool that can actually observe the mind. You can observe your mind. I don’t even know if you have thoughts.”
Dr. K
02 — Knowledge vs Wisdom
Vidya vs Gñán: Information Doesn’t Change You
“Information doesn’t change behavior. The moment that you have gñán, the moment that you understand something, you have no choice but to change.”
Dr. K
📖

Vidya = Information

Objective, transmissible. "My car is parked over there." You can pass it along, but it doesn't transform anyone.

💡

Gñán = Understanding

Subjective, non-transmissible. Like a child tasting wasabi for the first time — after that experience, behavior permanently changes.

⚠️

The TLDR Trap

"I read 10 books on business habits. Here's the TLDR." — That goes into short-term memory and gets wiped when you go to bed. You have to play around with information to really learn it.

“The understanding doesn't exist in the book. If the understanding was in the book, anyone who read it would get the understanding. The understanding comes from within you — the book serves as a trigger.”
Dr. K on how knowledge actually works
03 — Attention
Your Attention Is Being Farmed
“You as a human being have one resource, which is probably the most valuable thing you have in your whole life, which is your attention. If you can focus your attention on a book, you will get an A. If you can focus your attention on work, you will make money.”
Dr. K
“The reclamation of our attention is essentially the reclamation of our life.”
Dr. K
📱

The Elevator Effect

Tech concentrates our attention for us. But the more we take the elevator, the weaker our legs get. We're outsourcing focus and atrophying the muscle.

🔁

The Opiate Cycle

Anxiety → can't control thoughts → pick up phone → temporary relief → weaker attention → more anxiety. Identical to the cycle of opiate addiction.

📊

Algorithmic Drift

Algorithms don't just steal your time. They shape your thoughts, your votes, your anger. "Online drift happens to everyone."

99.999%
“If you look at your life and your life sucks, there is a 99.999% chance that your thinking is habitual.”

Most of your thoughts are not from today. They are echoes of the past. If you’ve had the thought before, it’s a vasana — a mental habit, not a reflection of reality.

05 — Idle Time
The Spiritual Cost of Never Being Bored
“When your brain is idle, your brain is not idle. It’s doing emotional processing, consolidating things into memory. As the idle time of our mind decreases, we are no longer processing the emotions of every day.”
Dr. K
💤

Dreams as Digestion

Just as your gut pulls out good stuff and discards bad stuff from food, your brain does that with emotions during idle time and sleep.

📦

Emotions Pile Up

When we suppress emotions with our phone, they don't get processed — they accumulate. That's why societal depression, anxiety, and loneliness are exploding.

❤️

People Can't Fall in Love

"South Korea's birth rate is super low because people on some level don't have the capacity to fall in love anymore." Emotional capacity requires idle processing.

“One-pointedness of attention feels good. Complete one-pointedness of attention feels blissful. That’s why people love orgasm. There are ways to achieve that in your daily life — 45 minute orgasms without having sex. That’s what we call samadhi.”
Dr. K on the neuroscience of meditation
06 — The Framework
The 0 → 100 Model of Human Growth

−100 → 0  |  Healing Pathology

Depression, suicidality, cognitive distortions. This is a malfunction of the mind. Requires clinical treatment — therapy, possibly medication. Fix what is broken first.

0 → 50  |  Shadow Work

Reintegrating the parts of yourself you amputated to succeed. The sacrifices you made — undoing them. “Shadow work is about reintegrating the parts of ourselves that we lopped off in order to get as far as we have.”

50 → 75  |  Ego Work

Getting control of the ego (ahamkara). The slippery trap: “The thought that I’ve conquered my ego is ego. That’s its mechanism of tricking you.”

75 → 100  |  Spiritual Development

Karma dissolution, shakti accumulation, samadhi. Doing things with no expectation — not even a good expectation. True equanimity.

07 — A Critical Distinction
“You’re Not Depressed. You’re Unhappy.”
“Half the people who come into my office are not depressed, they’re unhappy, but the language that we use is depression.”
Dr. K
🏥

Depression = Pathology

Cognitive distortions. "My family would be better off if I was dead." A malfunction of the mind requiring clinical intervention.

😔

Unhappy = Not Sick

Functional, working well, no distortions — but unfulfilled. This doesn't need medication. It needs a life worth living.

🌍

Appropriate Sadness

"If you have a genuinely bad life and you are sad, that is not a malfunction of the mind. That is an appropriate reaction to your circumstances."

“99% of the karma that you and I do is mental. Thoughts have effects. Emotions have effects. Most of our thinking is just habitual — and breaking free of your patterns of thinking is literally how people become exceptional.”
Dr. K on karma & vasanas
08 — The Crisis
Why Young Men Are Spiritually Bereft
“The core of the problem is that men have become spiritually bereft. There’s no spirituality within men generally anymore.”
Dr. K
🧭

No Internal Compass

Men are alexithymic — colorblind to their emotional state. Without that compass, they copy other people's desires instead of finding their own.

💪

Discipline Won't Save Them

"They long for discipline. They want willpower. None of that stuff is going to save them." Without passion and internal direction, force fails.

🔥

What They Actually Need

"Men are looking for answers, but what they really need is direction. They need to find that internal direction and they're not connected to it."

09 — Dharma
Dharma: The Antidote to Willpower
“Dharma is what allows you to do hard things. If someone pulls a gun on my daughter, I’m stepping in the line of the gun. It becomes easy. It becomes simple. There’s no question.”
Dr. K
🚫

Shoulds vs Dharma

Shoulds come from societal conditioning & create anxiety. Dharma comes from within — it's a feeling, not a thought. "I don't have discipline. I don't have willpower. I don't think you need it."

⚖️

Wants & Shoulds Are Twins

"Once you get rid of the shoulds, the wants will go away too — because they're created as avoidance of a should." Both dissolve when dharma emerges.

🌶️

Poison Before Ambrosia

Like churning the ocean in Hindu mythology — poison comes up first. Finding your dharma starts with boredom and discomfort. On the other side is fulfillment.

“You will never become enlightened if you desire enlightenment. It doesn’t matter if you’re longing for enlightenment or longing for a car — it’s still longing. That’s still the problem.”
Dr. K on the paradox of striving
10 — Practices
Concrete Practices Dr. K Recommends
🕯️

Trataka (Candle Gazing)

Stare at a candle flame 1–5 min without blinking, then close eyes. Trains focus, feels challenging in a non-boring way, and secretly cleanses the ajna (third eye) chakra — increasing intuition.

💨

Anulom Vilom (Without Fingers)

Alternate nostril breathing using only attention — no hands. Visualize air moving in a triangle up to the third eye. The more subtle the practice, the more potent the spiritual effect.

🧘

Break Vasanas Daily

Ask yourself: "Have I had this thought before?" If yes, it's an echo — not reality. 99% of thinking is recycled. This one question breaks the loop.

⏸️

Reclaim Idle Time

Stop picking up your phone every idle moment. Let boredom exist. Your brain uses that time for emotional processing, memory consolidation, and accessing intuition.

11 — Shakti
The Irrigation Ditch: Why Spiritual Practice Seems Futile Until It Isn’t
“Imagine I have a field and there’s a river. I start digging an irrigation ditch. The first 99 feet I dig, there’s zero water irrigating the field. That last bit of earth — suddenly one unit of effort irrigates my entire field. Even when the 99 units of effort irrigated at 0%.”
Dr. K on shakti accumulation
⚡

Non-Linear Progress

Spiritual energy (shakti) accumulates invisibly, then hits a critical threshold where life suddenly starts going well. This is why most people quit before the breakthrough.

💰

Compound Spiritual Interest

Like compound interest in a bank account. But if you start spending it (externalizing siddhis / psychic powers), your balance drains rapidly.

“To know thyself is to sit with that which has no object but still exists. You’re not your body — I can take a piece off and you’re still you. You’re not your mind — it changes over and over. The one permanent part is that you experience your life.”
Dr. K — defining who we are at our essence
12 — Enlightenment
Samadhi, Moksha & the Nature of Enlightenment
“Experiences of samadhi radically transform your relationship with yourself and your relationship with the outside world. You begin to realize that we’re all living in the matrix — which lets you chill out intensely.”
Dr. K
🌟

Samadhi = Temporary Enlightenment

Bliss + freedom + knowledge. Waves of orgasmic bliss for extended periods. Default mode network completely deactivated. Ego dissolved.

♾️

Moksha = One Moment Forever

"It doesn't actually have duration. It's just one moment duplicated again and again. Not for infinity — just this moment, this moment, this moment."

🧪

Dopamine vs Serotonin

Happiness from external things = dopamine (builds tolerance, never enough). Contentment from within = serotonin. They're inversely related. Enlightenment is serotonergic.

“We’re trapped, so we might as well make some sexually explicit jokes and have a good time and meditate a little bit. That’s what it is.”

Dr. K — closing wisdom
